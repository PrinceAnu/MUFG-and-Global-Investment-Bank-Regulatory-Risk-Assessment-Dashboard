AML / sanctions violations CRITICAL

The single biggest driver of bank fines globally. Over $45bn since 2000 across HSBC, BNP Paribas, Standard Chartered, TD Bank, Commerzbank. US DoJ leads (59% of AML value); FCA, DNB, AUSTRAC increasingly active. Iran, Russia, Cuba, Sudan the key sanction regimes.

Key regulators: DoJ, OFAC, FinCEN, FCA, DNB, AUSTRAC

Benchmark / rate manipulation CRITICAL

LIBOR, EURIBOR, FX, ISDAfix and commodity benchmarks. Coordinated trader misconduct across Deutsche Bank (£226.8m FCA), Barclays, UBS, RBS, Citi, JPMorgan. FCA, DoJ, CFTC and EU Commission all active. MAR/BMR now codify expected controls.

Key regulators: FCA, CFTC, DoJ, EU Commission

FX / market misconduct HIGH

Cartel coordination in G10 FX spot, front-running, improper disclosure of client flow. "The Cartel" and "The Bankers Club" chat rooms. $11bn+ across Citi, JPM, Barclays, HSBC, RBS, UBS. MUFG's own FX investigation is ongoing and referenced in their 6-K filings.

Key regulators: DoJ, CFTC, FCA, EU Commission

Market abuse / insider trading HIGH

Persistent FCA and SEC priority. MNPI leakage, front-running, improper wall-crossing, spoofing and layering. Equities, fixed income, derivatives. 32 insider trading SEC actions in FY2023 alone. FCA enforcement up 230% in value in 2024 vs 2023.

Key regulators: FCA, SEC, ESMA NCAs, CFTC

Regulatory reporting / transparency HIGH

MiFID II trade and transaction reporting, EMIR, SFTR, Dodd-Frank swap reporting, CFTC recordkeeping. Widespread industry failures. CFTC alone $1.1bn in CMPs across 20 FIs for recordkeeping failings (FY2022–23). Off-channel comms $3.6bn across 100+ firms.

Key regulators: FCA, ESMA, CFTC, SEC

Consumer / mortgage / conduct MEDIUM

MBS misselling ($40bn+, US-focused), PPI UK, fee deception (MUFG Union Bank $15m OCC fine 2023), unsuitable products. Consumer Duty (UK 2023) raises expectations. Less relevant for a wholesale IB but material for MUFG's retail and wealth arms.

Key regulators: OCC, FCA, CFPB, FINRA

Operational / cyber / resilience MEDIUM

DORA (EU, 2025), FCA/PRA operational resilience rules. Third-party risk concentration, outsourcing failures, IT outages. Growing enforcement focus. BoE/FCA systemic designation rules apply to MUFG as a G-SIB and UK-regulated entity.

Key regulators: PRA, FCA, DORA (ESMA/EBA/EIOPA)

Tax / transfer pricing evasion LOW–MED

Dividend stripping (cum-ex), structured tax schemes, Luxembourg/Ireland tax optimization products. French DoJ and BaFin increasingly active in 2025. Cross-border structures through MUFG's Luxembourg entity and Japan trust banking arms carry latent exposure.

Key regulators: HMRC, French PNF, DoJ, German BaFin

Information barrier / MNPI failures TOP RISK

Evidenced directly: Japan FSA found 26+ instances of improper client information sharing between MUFG Bank and MUMSS/MSMS (Jun 2024). Structural conflict between banking and securities JV (Morgan Stanley alliance) makes this endemic. MUFG Bank seeking underwriting business for its brokerage arm — a classic MNPI/Chinese wall violation.

Regulator: Japan FSA/SESC — Business improvement orders, Jun 2024

Regulatory reporting / substituted compliance TOP RISK

MUFG Securities EMEA fined $9.8m (SEC, Aug 2025) for 3+ years of recordkeeping, capital reporting, financial reporting and internal supervision failures as an SBSD. The firm submitted post-hoc records after an enforcement investigation began. Fabricated compliance attestations. Highly relevant to MUFG EMEA's cross-border swap book and reporting obligations.

Regulator: SEC — $9.8m fine, Aug 2025

FX market conduct / coordination HIGH

MUFG's own 20-F and 6-K filings confirm "some investigations into foreign exchange related practices resulted in our payment of monetary penalties." Investigation described as ongoing and subject to potential additional regulatory actions. MUFG's global FX franchise spans bank, securities EMEA/Americas — all in scope.

Regulators: DoJ, CFTC — investigation ongoing per filings

Client data & confidentiality HIGH

Japan FSA enforcement (Jun 2024) was specifically about misuse of confidential client data to win business. This touches GDPR (EU entities), UK GDPR, Japan's APPI and MiFID II client confidentiality obligations simultaneously. Especially acute where MUFG Bank, MUMSS and MUFG EMEA share client universe across jurisdictions.

Regulators: Japan FSA, FCA, FSMA/EU privacy regulators

OFAC / sanctions compliance HIGH

Historical: MUFG paid $565m in NYDFS sanctions fines (2013–14) for Iran/Sudan wire-stripping. OCC Consent Order (Nov 2017) and OCC C&D (Feb 2019) both OFAC/BSA-driven. As a G-SIB with large USD clearing, Southeast Asian affiliate network (Thailand, Indonesia, Philippines, Vietnam), and Japan-headquartered ownership, sanctions exposure remains structurally elevated.

Regulators: OFAC, OCC, NYDFS — historical; latent risk ongoing

Supervisory transparency / regulator communication HIGH

PRA fined MUFG £26.8m (2017) for failing to notify the PRA of DFS settlement discussions. SEC enforcement (2025) found MUFG admitted non-compliance, provided inaccurate records, then fabricated post-hoc records after investigation began. Two separate instances across different entities of poor regulator candour — a pattern risk.

Regulators: PRA (2017), SEC (2025)

Off-channel communications MEDIUM

Industry-wide SEC/CFTC sweep has caught 100+ firms. MUFG Securities Americas was designated a Primary Dealer in Jan 2026 — increasing its regulatory profile for CFTC and FINRA comms surveillance. No confirmed MUFG-specific fine yet in this wave, but the risk profile of its US broker-dealer and EMEA swap dealer makes it a candidate.

Regulators: SEC, CFTC, FCA — industry sweep active

Spoofing / algorithmic market manipulation MEDIUM

CFTC fined BTMU $600k for spoofing of Treasury futures / Eurodollar futures (Aug 2017) — self-reported. MUFG Securities Americas now a Primary Dealer in USTs (Jan 2026), increasing its CFTC surveillance footprint. Fixed income, rates and repo flows across MUFG Bank treasury and the securities entities create latent spoofing/layering risk.

Regulators: CFTC, SEC — heightened given Primary Dealer status
